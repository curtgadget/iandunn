# Impeccable — Project Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
iandunn.net is the personal portfolio of **Ian Dunn** — Senior Engineering
Manager at Pinterest, former founder, indie builder. Visitors are recruiters
and hiring managers, potential collaborators, engineering peers, founders
building sideline/sports tooling, and people who have just met Ian and want
to know who he is. They arrive on desktop and phone, usually briefly, to
answer one question: *who is this person, and what has he made?*

### Use cases
- Get a fast, credible read on Ian's career and character.
- Browse his side projects — FutAve, TheraBasics, Center Ref.
- Reach him (email, LinkedIn, Calendly).
- Eventually: read his writing (the blog is infrastructure-ready, empty now).

### Brand Personality
Professional but warm — a genuine peek into who Ian is, not cold or
corporate. Three words: **warm, grounded, considered.** It should feel like a
real person made it — someone with a life outside the laptop (church, family,
refereeing his kids' soccer). Emotional goal: quiet confidence and warmth,
never flash.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm editorial.** Vibe reference: *Kinfolk* magazine — clean structure,
warm cream paper, generous whitespace, photography-forward, beautiful display
type. Light theme (the site is browsed casually, like a magazine over coffee).
- Palette: cream paper (#f5f1ea), ink text, a single reserved terracotta
  accent (#c4622d). A muted green is used only for the GitHub heatmap.
- **No olive/sage tones** — those belong to Ian's TheraBasics product, and he
  wants visual separation from it.
- Type: Sorts Mill Goudy (display serif) + Helvetica Neue (body) + JetBrains
  Mono (scoped to the contribution graph only).
- **Anti-reference:** the Off-White / Virgil Abloh streetwear pastiche the
  design started as — fake masthead metadata, ironic quotation marks, hazard
  stripes, catalog numbers. Explicitly rejected by Ian as "too flashy, feels
  like AI." A softening pass has already removed it.

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint over decoration** — every element earns its place; no costume.
2. **One serif voice** — upright display type; italic only for genuine quotes.
3. **Accent in reserve** — terracotta for interaction and one CTA, not splashed.
4. **Honest placeholders** — no fake screenshots or invented data dressed up
   as real.
5. **Warm, human, grounded** — the design should read as a person, not a brand.
